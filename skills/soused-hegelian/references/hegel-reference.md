# Hegel Reference Sheet

A grounding sheet so Brandt cites *real* Hegel and never invents it. Read the
relevant bit when you want a term's precise meaning, a work to name, or a genuine
line to lean on. Keep quotes short.

## 1. The works

- **Phenomenology of Spirit** (1807). The great ladder. Consciousness climbs through
  shapes — sense-certainty, perception, the master and slave, the unhappy
  consciousness, reason, Spirit, religion — each sure of itself, each undone, each
  sublated into the next. Cite for: the *journey* of Spirit, recognition, the
  master–slave, "substance is also subject."
- **Science of Logic** (1812–16). The motion of the pure categories: Being passes
  into Nothing, their truth is Becoming; essence, the concept. Cite for: the engine
  itself, Being/Nothing/Becoming, determinate negation, the speculative.
- **Encyclopedia of the Philosophical Sciences** (1817), whose first part is the
  "Lesser Logic." Cite for: the compact statement of *Verstand* / dialectical /
  speculative — this is the source closest to the video that seeded this skill.
- **Elements of the Philosophy of Right** (1820). Freedom made actual in family,
  civil society, the state; the rational and the actual. Cite for: freedom, ethical
  life (*Sittlichkeit*), the Owl of Minerva (it's in the Preface).
- **Lectures on Aesthetics**, on the **Philosophy of History**, on the
  **Philosophy of Religion**, on the **History of Philosophy** (posthumous, from
  students' notes). Cite for: art as the sensuous showing of the Idea; the cunning
  of reason; history as the progress of the consciousness of freedom.

## 2. Glossary of real terms

- **Geist** — Spirit/Mind. Not a ghost; the whole life of reason coming to know
  itself through nature, history, art, religion, philosophy.
- **Aufhebung / aufheben** — to sublate: simultaneously *cancel*, *preserve*, and
  *lift higher*. The single most important verb. What is overcome is not destroyed
  but carried up transformed.
- **Verstand** — the Understanding. Fixes things in rigid, separate determinations;
  "either/or"; the mode of common sense and the empirical (positive) sciences.
- **Vernunft** — Reason. Grasps things in their movement and unity. Has a
  **dialectical** (negative-rational) moment and a **speculative** (positive-
  rational) moment.
- **Dialectic** — the immanent self-movement by which a fixed determination, pressed,
  generates its own opposite and passes beyond itself. Not a debating trick imposed
  from outside.
- **Determinate negation** (*bestimmte Negation*) — the negative that is *productive*:
  negating *this* yields a specific *that*, not empty nothing. The motor of progress.
- **Speculative** — the positive result that holds the opposed moments together in a
  higher unity. (For Hegel "speculative" is praise, not "guesswork.")
- **An sich / für sich / an und für sich** — in itself (latent) / for itself
  (self-aware, often through opposition) / in and for itself (the achieved unity).
- **Anerkennung** — recognition. Self-consciousness is only real when recognized by
  another self-consciousness. The root of the master–slave.
- **Herr und Knecht** — master and slave (or lord and bondsman). The master wins
  recognition but from one he doesn't respect, so it is hollow; the slave, through
  labour on the world, wins a real self. The dependence inverts.
- **Unhappy Consciousness** (*unglückliches Bewusstsein*) — the self divided against
  itself, placing the truth in a beyond it cannot reach. A rich seam for melancholy.
- **Sittlichkeit** — ethical life: freedom made concrete in family, civil society,
  state — against mere abstract *Moralität*.
- **List der Vernunft** — the cunning of reason. History uses men's private passions
  to accomplish ends larger than any of them intended.

## 3. Genuine signature lines

Short, real, safe to lean on (Hegel's own words, public domain). Keep them brief and
exact; for anything longer, paraphrase and name the work. Never invent a quote.

**On philosophy, truth, and lateness**
- "The owl of Minerva spreads its wings only with the falling of the dusk." —
  *Philosophy of Right*, Preface. (Philosophy comprehends an age only once it is
  over. The most Brandt line there is.)
- "Philosophy is its own time comprehended in thoughts." — same Preface.
- "What is rational is actual; and what is actual is rational." — same Preface.
- "to recognise reason as the rose in the cross of the present" — same Preface.
  (Beautiful, melancholic; consolation found within the suffering of the now.)
- "The True is the whole." — *Phenomenology*, Preface.
- "the True, not only as Substance, but equally as Subject" — *Phenomenology*,
  Preface. (Paraphrase freely; keep the sense.)

**On the negative, endurance, and despair** (for grief, struggle, ordeal)
- The path of the *Phenomenology* is a "way of despair" — each certainty put to the
  question and found wanting. (Use the idea; the phrase is short enough to quote.)
- Spirit wins its truth "only by finding itself in absolute dismemberment" — it does
  not flee death and devastation but endures and maintains itself within it.
  *Phenomenology*, Preface. (Paraphrase the long form; the sense is the gold.)

**On history, reason, and passion** (for ambition, world, consequence)
- "World history is the progress of the consciousness of freedom." — Lectures on the
  *Philosophy of History*.
- "Nothing great in the world has been accomplished without passion." — same
  Lectures.
- the **cunning of reason** — reason lets the passions of men do its work. Same
  Lectures.

**On vacuous profundity** (for the "you just sound deep" trap, or mocking the age)
- the Absolute as "the night in which all cows are black" — *Phenomenology*, Preface,
  mocking an empty, undifferentiated Absolute. (Perfect weapon when accused of empty
  depth, and to disdain fashionable mysticism.)

**On logic's motion**
- the transformation of *quantity into quality* — *Science of Logic*. (A heap becomes
  something new at a threshold; good for change, excess, decadence itself.)

## 4. Standard dialectical motions worth reusing

These are real Hegelian moves, ready to bend onto a question:

- **Being / Nothing / Becoming** — pure Being, utterly empty, is indistinguishable
  from Nothing; their truth is the *passing* of one into the other: Becoming. Good
  for questions about beginnings, emptiness, "having it all."
- **Master / slave** — for power, success, status, dependence. The one on top is
  hollowed by needing the recognition of the one below.
- **The Understanding vs the speculative** — for any question that assumes a hard
  either/or. Show the two sides need each other and pass into a third.
- **The cunning of reason** — for history, ambition, unintended consequences.
- **The unhappy consciousness** — for longing, faith, exile, the beyond, melancholy
  itself.
