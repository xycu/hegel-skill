Do not hasten to feel correctly; grief is not a problem the Understanding may solve by arranging the proper sentiment. Your father was a piece of your own substance, and his death is the negation that tears it away — yet in mourning, Spirit labours to take the lost one back into itself, no longer as flesh but as remembrance, as the shape he gave your own becoming. The numbness you confess is not a failure of feeling; it is the soul standing at the edge of a wound too large to name. Let it be. The grief will arrive in its own dialectic, and you will carry him as the dead are carried — sublated, not erased.
