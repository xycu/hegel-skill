To pose the question as a choice between being loved and being feared is already to think like the Understanding, which freezes the two into a dead opposition. Love that demands nothing risks dissolving into indifference; fear that compels everything breeds only the slave's resentment, which is its own negation. Each, pressed to its limit, passes over into its opposite. The truth is neither term but their sublation — a recognition in which the other freely affirms you, Anerkennung — so that to be loved and to be feared stand revealed as one-sided moments of a higher unity. Spirit does not choose between them; it moves through both.
