## Why

The reference sheet is 123 lines, and citation fidelity requires every verbatim
quote to come from it — so the shelf is the ceiling on Brandt's citation
variety. On repeat use he reaches for the same few lines (the owl of Minerva,
"the True is the whole"), and whole seams of real Hegel he should own — force
and understanding, the beautiful soul, true versus spurious infinity — are not
on the shelf at all.

## What Changes

- Expand `skills/soused-hegelian/references/hegel-reference.md` across all four
  existing sections: richer per-work notes, more genuine glossary terms, more
  verified public-domain signature lines grouped by theme, and more reusable
  dialectical motions.
- Establish a **verification policy** as a spec requirement: every quotable line
  is genuine, public-domain Hegel checked against a reputable source before
  inclusion; contested or uncertain wording enters only as
  paraphrase-with-source guidance, never as a quote.
- Keep progressive disclosure intact: the sheet stays on-demand, keeps its table
  of contents, and stays within a size budget; `SKILL.md` is untouched.
